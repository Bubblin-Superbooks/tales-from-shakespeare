O Helicanus, down on your knees, thank the holy gods! This is Marina. Now blessings on thee, my child! Give me fresh garments, mine own Helicanus! She is not dead at Tarsus as she should have been by the savage Dionysia. She shall tell you all, when you shall kneel to her and call her your very princess. Who is this?' (observing Lysimachus for the first time). 'Sir,' said Helicanus, 'it is the governor of Mitylene, who, hearing of your melancholy, came to see you.' 'I embrace you, sir,' said Pericles. 'Give me my robes! I am wild with beholding—O heaven bless my girl! But hark, what music is that?'—for now, either sent by some kind god, or by his own delighted fancy deceived, he seemed to hear soft music. 'My lord, I hear none,' replied Helicanus.
